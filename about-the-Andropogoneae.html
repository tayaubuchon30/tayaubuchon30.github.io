About the Andropogoneae

Where are Andropogoneae?

Everywhere! The vast cornfields of midwestern North America replaced the tallgrass prairie that occupied the same land since the glaciers retreated. The massive grasslands of eastern and western Africa provide food for herbivores both large (wildebeest, zebras) and small (insects of all sorts) and were the home of the earliest mammals now called Homo sapiens. The tropical savannas of Australia and southeast Asia and the cerrado of South America all are ecosystems that rival the rainforests of those countries. What all these grasslands – including the corn (maize) – have in common is that the grasses are all members of a single evolutionary radiation, or clade, the grass tribe Andropogoneae.

How old is Andropogoneae?

The tribe is thought to have originated in the Eastern Hemisphere, possibly in southern or eastern Asia, in the Miocene, about 13 million years ago (9.89-17.97).
